# Impeccable Design Context — CocktailOS Portail

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary — Clients (portail side):**
Customers of Cocktail Média — local businesses and SMEs, often going through their first or early branding experience. They're not designers. They come to the portal to track project progress, upload documents when requested, check email-notified updates, and find deliverables from past projects. They check on mobile after receiving a notification email. They need to feel reassured and capable — not overwhelmed. **User-friendliness is the single most important emotional outcome.**

**Secondary — Admins / future Freelancers (admin side):**
Currently Cocktail Média staff managing clients, projects, invoices, and services. Long-term: the admin section evolves into a lightweight CRM + finance + inventory tool for freelancers in any field who want something affordable and simple. Power users who spend focused sessions in the tool.

**Job to be done (client):** "I want to see where my project is, do what's asked of me, and feel confident it's progressing."

**Job to be done (admin/freelancer):** "I need to manage multiple clients and projects without a bloated tool."

---

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** creative, accessible, simple

**Tone:** Direct and assured — speaks like a confident creative studio, not a corporate SaaS. Warm but not casual. Bilingual (French first, English later).

**Emotional goals:**
- Client side: *reassurance*, *clarity*, *quiet pride* (their brand is being taken care of)
- Admin side: *speed*, *control*, *focus*

---

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** High contrast. Bold typography. Colorful but restrained — one strong accent used with precision, not scattered everywhere. A deliberate mix of dark and light surfaces. Creative without being chaotic. Simple without being sterile.

**Theme:** Mixed — dark surfaces for login and admin (focused tool, professional context), warm light cream for client portal (approachable, reassuring, daytime use). NOT a simple light/dark toggle — the two sides of the product have genuinely different atmospheres.

**Typography:**
- Display / headings: **Bricolage Grotesque** (variable, Google Fonts) — slightly irregular humanist grotesk, feels creative and alive, confident at large weights
- Body / UI: **Atkinson Hyperlegible** (Google Fonts) — designed for readability and accessibility, distinctive without drawing attention to itself
- These replace the current Bebas Neue + Poppins pairing

**Typography system (portail client) — décisions 2026-05-14 :**
- H1 pages (dashboard, projet, identite-visuelle, decision-board) : `var(--text-3xl)` = 2.75rem, Bricolage 800, uppercase, `letterSpacing: '-0.025em'`, `lineHeight: 1.0`
- Exception : page Profil garde un grand `clamp(3rem, 10vw, 5rem)` pour le nom — page personnelle, effet voulu
- H2 sections interne (ex: profil) : `var(--text-2xl)` = 2rem, Bricolage
- Labels de section : `var(--text-xs)`, body 700, uppercase, `letterSpacing: '0.12em'`, couleur `light-text-3`
- Règle : toujours utiliser les tokens CSS (`var(--text-*)`) — jamais de valeurs rem hardcodées dans les composants

**Colors (OKLCH):**
- Brand accent: `oklch(52% 0.21 32)` — the existing Cocktail Média orange-red (#e83b14), preserved
- Dark surface: `oklch(18% 0.015 35)` — near-black with warm tint toward brand
- Light surface: `oklch(97% 0.012 70)` — warm cream tinted toward brand
- Text on dark: `oklch(92% 0.02 70)` — warm near-white
- Text on light: `oklch(22% 0.015 35)` — warm near-black
- Borders (dark context): `oklch(30% 0.01 35)` at 60% opacity
- Borders (light context): `oklch(80% 0.015 70)` solid

**Anti-references:** Generic SaaS dashboards (same card grid, hero metrics, gradient text). "AI slop" — glassmorphism for the sake of it, glow effects, cyan-on-dark. Agency portfolios that are all flash with no usability. Anything that makes a non-technical client feel lost.

**References feel:** The precision of a well-designed print invoice. The calm authority of a good editorial layout. Something a client receives and thinks "these people know what they're doing."

---

### Design Principles

1. **User-friendliness is the brief.** Every decision that creates friction for the client is wrong, regardless of how good it looks. If something looks amazing but confuses a non-designer, redesign it.

2. **High contrast, earned color.** The orange-red accent appears where it matters — primary actions, active states, key statuses. Everywhere else: neutrals do the work. Color is the exception, not the rule.

3. **Bold type, quiet layout.** Typography carries the visual weight. Layout breathes. No card grids, no identical sections, no decorative icons above every heading.

4. **Two distinct atmospheres.** Client portal = warm, light, calm. Admin/freelancer = dark, structured, efficient. These are not just theme swaps — they have different spacing density, different motion energy, different information hierarchy.

5. **Future-ready simplicity.** The UI must accommodate a future where the admin side becomes a multi-tenant freelancer tool. That means clear component boundaries, text in constants (i18n-ready), and no layout patterns that only work for one use case.

---

### Technical Constraints

- **Stack:** Next.js 16, React 19, TypeScript, TailwindCSS v4 — no global state manager
- **Font delivery:** Google Fonts via `next/font` (not raw `<link>` tags in layout)
- **Accessibility:** WCAG AA minimum — 4.5:1 body contrast, 3:1 large text/UI components
- **Motion:** All animations must respect `prefers-reduced-motion` with a static fallback
- **Touch:** Interactive targets ≥ 44px — clients check on mobile after email notifications
- **Keyboard:** Full keyboard navigation + visible focus states, especially in admin
- **Language:** French-first; text extracted to constants for future EN translation (no hardcoded strings inline in components long-term)
- **Icons:** Material Symbols Outlined already loaded — continue using for consistency
